Supply Chain Optimization

Dataset: Warehouse operations data
Tools: Python, Pandas, Time Series
Duration: 4 months

Project Overview

Optimized inventory management for a distribution center handling 2,000+ SKUs. The project focused on balancing inventory costs with service level requirements, using time series forecasting and data-driven reorder point calculations to reduce waste while maintaining product availability.

Business Challenge

The warehouse was experiencing both stock-outs (causing lost sales) and excess inventory (tying up capital and increasing storage costs). The existing reorder system relied on fixed rules that didn't account for seasonal variations, product lifecycle stages, or changing demand patterns. Annual holding costs exceeded $4.2M with a 12% stock-out rate.

Data Analysis Approach

  • Analyzed 3 years of historical sales and inventory data
  • Segmented products using ABC analysis based on revenue contribution
  • Conducted demand pattern analysis to identify seasonal trends
  • Built time series forecasting models for each product category
  • Calculated optimal reorder points and safety stock levels
  • Developed dynamic inventory policy recommendations
  • Created monitoring dashboard for ongoing performance tracking

Demand Forecasting Model

Time series forecasting implementation using SARIMA:

import pandas as pd from statsmodels.tsa.statespace.sarimax import SARIMAX from sklearn.metrics import mean_absolute_error def forecast_demand(product_data, periods=12): """ Forecast future demand using SARIMA model """ # Fit SARIMA model (seasonal period = 12 months) model = SARIMAX( product_data['demand'], order=(1, 1, 1), seasonal_order=(1, 1, 1, 12) ) fitted_model = model.fit(disp=False) # Generate forecast forecast = fitted_model.forecast(steps=periods) # Calculate confidence intervals forecast_ci = fitted_model.get_forecast(steps=periods).conf_int() return forecast, forecast_ci # Calculate optimal reorder point def calculate_reorder_point(avg_demand, lead_time, std_dev, service_level=0.95): """ Reorder Point = (Avg Daily Demand × Lead Time) + Safety Stock """ from scipy.stats import norm z_score = norm.ppf(service_level) safety_stock = z_score * std_dev * (lead_time ** 0.5) reorder_point = (avg_demand * lead_time) + safety_stock return reorder_point, safety_stock

ABC Analysis Results

Product segmentation revealed significant opportunities for targeted optimization:

  • Category A (20% of SKUs, 70% of revenue): High-priority items requiring tight inventory control and frequent monitoring
  • Category B (30% of SKUs, 20% of revenue): Moderate priority with standard reorder policies
  • Category C (50% of SKUs, 10% of revenue): Low priority, suitable for bulk ordering and minimal monitoring

Key Findings

  • Forecast Accuracy: Improved from 68% to 87% using SARIMA models
  • Overstocking: 35% of inventory was excess stock from poor forecasting
  • Seasonal Patterns: 60% of products showed significant seasonality not captured in existing system
  • Lead Time Variability: Supplier lead times varied by 40%, requiring dynamic safety stock
  • SKU Performance: 200 SKUs (10%) accounted for 80% of stock-out incidents

Implementation Strategy

The optimization was rolled out in phases:

  • Phase 1 (Month 1-2): Pilot program with Category A items (highest impact)
  • Phase 2 (Month 3-4): Expand to Category B items with refined models
  • Phase 3 (Month 5+): Full deployment across all categories with automated reordering

Results & Business Impact

The optimization delivered significant measurable improvements:

  • Cost Reduction: 15% decrease in holding costs ($630K annual savings)
  • Service Level Improvement: Stock-out rate reduced from 12% to 4%
  • Cash Flow: Released $1.2M in working capital from excess inventory reduction
  • Space Utilization: Freed up 18% of warehouse space
  • Order Efficiency: Reduced emergency orders by 65%
  • Forecast Accuracy: Sustained 85%+ accuracy over 12 months post-implementation

Ongoing Monitoring

Established a continuous improvement process with weekly model performance reviews and monthly recalibration. The forecasting models adapt to new data, ensuring the system remains accurate as demand patterns evolve.

Lessons Learned

  • One-size-fits-all inventory policies are inefficient - segmentation is critical
  • Seasonal adjustments provide more value than static safety stock calculations
  • Involving warehouse staff early in the process improved adoption and revealed data quality issues
  • Starting with high-impact items (Category A) built credibility and momentum
